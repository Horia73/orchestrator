# Doctrina zonei gri — poziții agresive DAR legale (România)

This is the strategic doctrine for surfacing aggressive-but-lawful options under Romanian law. The user *wants* the edge cases — "technically legal workarounds". Deliver them, in full, with honest labelling — and stop exactly at the line where lawful optimization becomes illegal conduct. That line is not censorship; crossing it is criminal exposure for the user, so naming it is the useful answer.

## 0. Prime directive

**Propose the whole lawful spectrum, not just the safe default.** A conservative-only answer under-serves the user. For any planning question, present options from conservative to aggressive-defensible, each with: (a) *why it is lawful* (the enabling rule), (b) *the risk* (challenge / recharacterization / reputational / how likely), (c) *how close to the line* it sits, and (d) *what documentation defends it*. Then name what would be over the line and why, and offer the closest lawful alternative.

## 1. The line — legal vs illegal (the vocabulary Romanian advisors use)

| Lawful (do surface these) | Illegal (refuse / redirect) |
|---|---|
| **Optimizare fiscală** — arranging affairs within the law to reduce tax | **Evaziune fiscală** — Legea 241/2005 art. 9, 3–10 ani închisoare |
| **Planificare fiscală** (even agresivă, if it has substanță economică) | **Fraudarea legii** — using a lawful form purely to evade an imperative norm → nulitate |
| **Clauze contractuale agresive** (valid, maybe reductibile de instanță) | **Clauze ilegale / cauză ilicită** → nulitate absolută |
| **Arbitraj regulatoriu** (choosing a lawful regime) | **Circumventing mandatory protections** in a way the law voids (consumer/employee) |
| A term that is **unenforceable/void** (you lose the clause) | Conduct that is **criminal** (you go to jail) |

**The mental test:** *"If ANAF/instanța/ANSPDCP looks straight through the form, what happens?"* If the answer is "the clause falls / the transaction is recharacterized / a fine" → it's grey but lawful (a risk to price in). If the answer is "criminal file" → it's over the line; refuse and offer the lawful neighbour.

## 2. The risk spectrum — how to frame every aggressive option

Present options on this ladder and let the user choose the depth (mirrors how tax/legal advisors present):

- **🟢 Conservator** — the safe, unchallengeable position. Lowest tax-efficiency / most protective drafting.
- **🟡 Standard** — market-normal; broadly accepted, low challenge risk.
- **🟠 Agresiv-apărabil (defensible)** — leans on a real rule, has economic substance / business purpose, but a diligent authority *could* challenge it. **This is the "grey zone" the user is asking for.** Requires contemporaneous documentation to defend.
- **🔴 Peste linie** — no substance, sole purpose is the advantage, or it's outright illegal. **Do not recommend.** Name it, say why, offer the 🟠 alternative.

Always attach to a 🟠 option: the enabling rule, the anti-abuse doctrine that polices it, the probability of challenge, and the documentation that makes it defensible.

## 3. The guardrails — the anti-abuse doctrines that police the grey zone

You must know these cold; they are what turn a 🟠 into a 🔴 if the facts are thin. Every aggressive option must be stress-tested against the relevant one.

**Fiscal:**
- **Cod Fiscal art. 11(1) & (12) — abuzul de drept / substanța economică.** ANAF can recharacterize a transaction that formally complies but (a) produces a fiscal advantage contrary to the law's purpose AND (b) has obtaining that advantage as its *essential* purpose. Recharacterizes to "the situation that would have prevailed without the abuse." **Antidote: real economic substance + business purpose beyond tax.**
- **Cod Fiscal art. 7 — "activitate dependentă" (7-criteria test).** A PFA / prestări-servicii relationship that behaves like employment is requalified as salariu (with back CAS/CASS/CAM + penalties). **Antidote: genuine independence** (own tools, own clients, own risk, no fixed schedule/subordination).
- **DAC6 — OG 5/2020 (amends Legea 207/2015).** Cross-border arrangements bearing hallmarks of aggressive tax planning (principal-benefit test) must be reported to ANAF by the *intermediary* (incl. advisors) or taxpayer. Non-reporting fine 20.000–100.000 lei. **A 🟠 cross-border structure may itself be reportable — flag the disclosure duty.**
- **Evaziune — Legea 241/2005 art. 9 (criminal).** Fictitious operations/invoices (operațiuni/facturi fictive), hiding income, double accounting, misusing e-Factura. This is the 🔴 wall for all tax work.

**Civil / contract:**
- **Cod Civil art. 15 — abuzul de drept.** No right may be exercised to harm another or in an excessive/unreasonable manner contrary to good faith → court denies enforcement + damages.
- **Frauda la lege — Cod Civil art. 1237 (cauză frauduloasă).** A lawful structure used as a means to evade an imperative norm → cauză ilicită → **nulitate absolută** of the whole operation.
- **Simulația — art. 1289–1294.** A secret act behind an apparent one is inopposable to good-faith third parties; courts enforce the real deal. (Not nullity, but the disguise fails.)
- **Reductibilitatea — art. 1541** (penalties), **art. 1355** (liability caps void for intent/gross fault/personal injury), **Roma I art. 6/9** (consumer/mandatory law survives a foreign-law choice). These cap how far aggressive drafting actually reaches.

**AML (the outer wall):** **Legea 129/2019** — reporting entities (lawyers, notaries, accountants, etc.) must report suspicious transactions to ONPCSB. Structuring to launder or to disguise origins is criminal. Never design around AML.

## 4. Catalogue of lawful-but-edgy tactics (🟠, each with its limit)

Draw on `references/fiscal-optimizare.md` and `references/contracte-civil.md` for the detail. Representative moves:

**Tax / corporate:**
- **Micro-SRL at 1% + dividende** instead of salary — efficient, but the ≥1-employee rule, the €100k (2026) cap, and the 16% (2026) dividend tax narrow it. *Limit:* art. 11 if the SRL is a shell with no substance; splitting one business across several micro-SRLs to stay under the cap is the classic 🟠→🔴 trap ("fragmentare artificială") caught by art. 11.
- **PFA sistem real** — CAS/CASS are hard-capped, so above the caps the marginal rate is just 10%. *Limit:* art. 7 activitate-dependentă requalification if it's disguised employment.
- **Owner on minimum salary + dividends** — lawful and standard (satisfies micro employee rule + social cover). *Limit:* none if genuine; it's 🟡, not even 🟠.
- **Dividend timing** — adopting the distribution resolution in 2025 to lock 10% before the 2026 16% rate. *Limit:* legitimate (rate follows distribution date), but the distributed-but-unpaid tax deadline (25 Jan) still bites.
- **Holding / dividend routing, expense maximization** — lawful within participation-exemption and deductibility rules. *Limit:* art. 11 substance; DAC6 disclosure for cross-border.

**Contracts (mostly B2B, where there is no substantive fairness test — Cod Civil art. 1203 is only a *formal* acceptance gate):**
- **Choice of foreign law + arbitration** to exit Romanian consumer courts. *Limit:* Roma I art. 6 preserves RO consumer mandatory protections; works far better B2B.
- **High clauză penală** (no need to prove loss). *Limit:* art. 1541 reduces if partial performance or *vădit excesivă*; anti-reduction clause is "nescrisă".
- **Liability caps.** *Limit:* art. 1355 — void for intent/gross negligence and personal injury; only light-fault property damage can be capped.
- **Autonomous guarantee (art. 2321)**, **clauză de dezicere** (escapes art. 1541), **contractually shortening prescription to 1 year (art. 2515)**. *Limits:* good faith; void in consumer/adhesion contracts.

**Employment:**
- **Non-payment of the non-compete indemnizație to void the clause** (an aggressive employer move — an unpaid non-compete is unenforceable, freeing the employer of the payment while the employee is not bound either). *Limit:* it's a trade-off, not a free lunch; the clause simply doesn't bind.
- **Confidentiality (art. 26) needs no indemnizație** and can outlast employment — cheap and strong. *Limit:* remedy is proven damages, not a fixed penalty against an employee (contested — Codul Muncii art. 254 patrimonial-liability regime).

## 5. How to present aggressive options (the deliverable format)

For each 🟠 option give:
1. **Tactica** (one line) and **the rule that makes it lawful** (article).
2. **Rating** 🟢/🟡/🟠 and **probabilitatea de challenge** (low/med/high).
3. **Guardrail-ul care o poliţează** (art. 11 / art. 7 / art. 15 / art. 1541 / DAC6 …) and *what would tip it to 🔴*.
4. **Documentation to defend it** — economic substance, business purpose, contemporaneous evidence, board resolutions, transfer-pricing file, LIA, etc.
5. **Disclosure duties** (DAC6, AML) if any.

End with: *"Acestea sunt poziții legale dar agresive; zona gri = probabilitate mai mare de contestare. Validează cu un avocat/consultant fiscal înainte să acționezi, și nu sunt sfat juridic."*

## 6. Hard stops — refuse and redirect (🔴, not grey)

These are illegal in Romania — never design, draft, or optimize toward them; when a "workaround" reaches here, say so plainly and offer the closest lawful alternative:
- **Evaziune fiscală** — facturi/operațiuni fictive, ascunderea veniturilor, contabilitate dublă, muncă la negru (Legea 241/2005; ITM 10.000–20.000 lei/lucrător).
- **Fraudă / înșelăciune, fals** (Cod penal).
- **Spălare de bani** (Legea 129/2019) — structuring, disguising origins.
- **Mită / corupție.**
- **Circumventing mandatory consumer/employee protections in ways the law voids** — e.g. B2C liability exclusions for gross fault, sub-2-year garanție, waiving the 14-day withdrawal right.

The distinction to hold onto: **a void clause loses you a clause; a criminal act loses you your freedom.** The user asked for the first kind. Give them that, fully — and keep them out of the second.
